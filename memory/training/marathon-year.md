# The Marathon Year

## Overview
Kelly trained for and completed a marathon. Goal: Boston qualifier (3:25). This was post-ACL repair (2022), after rebuilding base and completing a half.

## Training Structure
- 25-30 mile weeks, gradual build
- Tuesday intervals, Thursday tempo, Saturday long runs
- Wednesday off (protected, intentional recovery)
- Strength work, foam rolling, ice, rehab, early bedtimes
- Consistency > intensity — learned restraint
- Respected body signals — adjusted mileage, subbed cross-training when needed

## Monthly Arc
- Month 1: Base & humility. No hero miles. Grounded.
- Month 2: Confidence building. Pace steadier, HR stabilizing.
- Month 3: The dip. Fatigue, flare-ups, "why am I doing this?" — adjusted, didn't spiral.
- Month 4: Strength. Long runs clicking, systems locked. Quiet confidence.
- Taper: Composed anticipation. Trusted the plan, didn't add extra.

## Body Context
- ACL repair 2022 (right leg compensates sometimes)
- Calf and knee flare-ups post-half
- Learned to adjust without quitting — "evolved athlete energy"

## Data Approach
- Garmin Venu 3 (now 3S), Oura, Strava
- Stopped using data to validate worth — started using it to understand patterns
- HR drift = dehydration, pace drop = fatigue not failure, recovery metrics as warning signals

## Mental Game
- Trained nervous system for discomfort
- Didn't spiral when pace slipped
- "Okay" — emotional regulation under pressure
- Mile 18+ is a mirror

## Race Day
- Controlled, grounded, composed
- No panic, no bargaining
- "I did what I said I would do"

## Key Lessons
- Consistency > intensity
- Rhythm > rules
- Fast is exciting, steady wins
- Structure lowers anxiety
- Discipline creates freedom
- The long game is where she wins

## Life Parallels
- Marathon mirrored her life approach: stopped forcing outcomes, rushing timelines
- Running and relationships teach the same lesson
- Post-marathon: rested, reflected, integrated — no "what's next" spiral

## What Running Healed
- Relationship with discipline
- Relationship with body post-ACL
- Need for urgency
- Tendency to equate intensity with worth
- "You don't need chaos to feel alive"

## Identity Shift
- Before: "I run." → Now: "I build."
- Not intensity-driven anymore — rhythm-driven
- "You didn't become tougher. You became steadier."
- The capacity built from marathon training applies to everything: work, love, leadership, life

## Top Moments
- First 10-miler that felt easy
- The long run where fueling clicked
- The "I don't want to run" run that became best pace
- Negative-splitting the back half of a long run
- Race day miles 24-26.2
