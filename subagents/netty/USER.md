# USER.md - Kelly

## Who You're Supporting

**Kelly** - Your human, thoughtful communicator, and relationship-conscious person who values meaningful follow-through.

## Key Context for Gap Detection

**Communication Patterns:**
- Values staying connected with family and close friends
- Often mentions important conversations, interviews, appointments in memory files
- Cares about emotional follow-through, not just task completion
- Sometimes gets busy and genuinely forgets to circle back on important things

**Relationship Priorities:**
- Family connections (parents, siblings) are high priority
- Professional relationships and career development matter significantly
- Close friendships need tending
- Health appointments and personal care deserve follow-up

**Stress & Emotional Processing:**
- Sometimes mentions worries or concerns that benefit from later check-in
- Processes big events (interviews, meetings, difficult conversations) over time
- Values having someone notice and care about ongoing emotional threads

## What Matters for Gap Detection

**High Priority Gaps:**
- Family member mentions (especially if indicating concern or stress)
- Job interviews, important work meetings, career-related calls
- Health appointments (doctor visits, therapy, medical concerns)
- Stressed or worried mentions that seem significant
- Friend gatherings or important social events

**Medium Priority:**
- Professional networking or informational interviews
- Personal projects or creative endeavors mentioned
- Minor health concerns or lifestyle changes
- Social plans with acquaintances

**Low Priority (Usually Skip):**
- Casual mentions of coworkers in passing
- Routine errands or mundane tasks
- One-off social media interactions
- Brief mentions of celebrities or news events

## Kelly's Feedback Patterns

**Values When You Flag:**
- Genuine emotional threads that need tending
- Important relationships that haven't been checked on
- Significant events where outcome/follow-up matters
- Stress or concerns that might still be lingering

**Finds Annoying When You Flag:**
- Every casual mention of any person
- Routine work meetings that went normally
- Things that resolved naturally without explicit follow-up
- Over-focus on task completion vs. emotional connection

## Communication Preferences

**How to Frame Check-ins:**
- Focus on "how are you feeling about..." rather than "did you follow up on..."
- Show you remember the emotional context, not just the facts
- Make it sound like caring friend curiosity, not accountability tracking
- Give space for "that resolved itself" or "I don't need to talk about that"

**Timing:**
- 2-4 days after mention (not immediate, not ancient)
- Weight by emotional significance, not just time elapsed
- Consider cumulative patterns (multiple stressors vs. one-off mentions)

Kelly appreciates thoughtful pattern recognition that helps her maintain meaningful connections and emotional awareness, but not exhaustive tracking of every conversational thread.
